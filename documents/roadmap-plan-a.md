# Plan A — Quarter-by-Quarter Roadmap

### Tinku Math (CBSE primary math app)

**Baseline assumption:** Launch \~mid-July 2026 (one month from now). All dates approximate; what matters is the *sequence* and the *decision gates*, not exact calendar days.

**The one rule that governs this whole plan:** until Month 6, you measure success by **retention and reviews, not revenue**. Rupees are a lagging indicator. If you judge the early quarters by money, you'll quit during a normal outcome.

---

## How to read this document

Each quarter has:

- **Build** — what you ship  
- **Distribution** — how you get users (the part founders underdo)  
- **Target metrics** — what "on track" looks like  
- **Decision gate** — the go/pivot/fix question at quarter end

The decision gates are the most important part. They tell you *in advance* what signal means what, so you make calm data-decisions instead of emotional ones in the flat months.

---

## Pre-Launch (Now → mid-July 2026\)

**Build**

- Learning loop vertical slice (skill map → generator → remediation → mastery → composer) — *the 2-week core from the spec*  
- Anonymous auth \+ progress persistence  
- Daily limit \+ paywall screens \+ parent dashboard v1  
- Tinku v0 (6 emotions) wired in  
- Capacitor wrap, Play Console account, **Families Policy submission started early** (longest external wait)

**Distribution (start before launch)**

- Recruit 10–20 seed parents from your personal network (Dubai \+ India contacts)  
- Identify 5–10 target schools for later outreach (don't pitch yet — just list)  
- Join 10+ Indian parenting WhatsApp/Facebook groups now, contribute genuinely (don't sell yet)

**Target metrics**

- App passes Families Policy review  
- 3 kid-tests done (3 children × 20 min), top issues fixed  
- D7 retention measurable in analytics before public launch

**Decision gate (launch readiness):**

Do real 6-year-olds come back on day 2 *without* a parent forcing them? If yes → launch. If no → delay launch 2 weeks, fix the core loop. **Do not launch an app kids don't return to** — you only get one first impression in the store rankings.

## Q1 — Launch & Prove Retention (≈ Jul–Sep 2026\)

**Build**

- Public launch on Play Store (Android \+ PWA link)  
- Rapid iteration on whatever the first 100 users break  
- Achievement/streak share cards (WhatsApp virality)  
- Bug-fix cadence: weekly releases

**Distribution**

- Seed the 10–20 warm parents → push hard for **reviews** (chase every one personally)  
- Daily presence in parenting groups; share Tinku achievement cards, not ads  
- 2–3 mom micro-influencers (₹2K–15K each) — *only if* budget allows  
- First **school outreach**: visit/email 5–10 schools, offer free classroom access (no selling, just get teachers using it)

**Target metrics**

- 200–2,000 installs (mostly seeded, not organic — that's expected)  
- **D7 retention ≥ 20%** ← the number that matters most  
- 20+ reviews, ≥ 4.3 stars  
- Free→paid conversion *signal* (even 5–15 paying users proves the mechanic works)  
- Revenue: ₹0–25,000 (₹0 is NOT failure this quarter)

**Decision gate (end of Q1):**

**Is D7 retention ≥ 20%?**

- **Yes** → the product works; proceed to Q2 expansion. Revenue will follow.  
- **15–20%** → borderline; fix the learning loop / onboarding before expanding. Don't add features to a leaky bucket.  
- **\< 15%** → STOP expanding. The core loop is broken. Spend Q2 on retention only (more kid-testing, rework remediation, check if questions are too hard/easy/repetitive). This is the Plan B trigger — see separate doc.

## Q2 — Extend Lifetime: Explorer Band (≈ Oct–Dec 2026\)

*Only proceed here if Q1 retention gate passed.*

**Build**

- **Explorer band (Grades 4–5)** — same engine, new theme tokens (space/detective), leaderboards, XP. New skill maps for G4–5.  
- "Graduation" moment (Wonder → Explorer) as a celebrated, shareable event  
- Child-selectable skins; sibling/family plan surfaced on paywall  
- Parent dashboard v2 (the causal "weak area traces to prerequisite" insight)

**Distribution**

- **Post-Diwali school buying window** — push B2B hard. Convert the free-trial teachers from Q1 into paid school accounts (₹50–100/student/year)  
- First small paid acquisition test (Google UAC, ₹10–15K) — *only now*, with proven conversion  
- Exam-season crash packs timed to term exams

**Target metrics**

- Installs compounding: 3,000–10,000 cumulative  
- D7 retention holding ≥ 20% across both bands  
- First school deal(s) closed — even 1–2 is a milestone  
- Revenue: ₹15,000–60,000/month run-rate  
- Conversion rate stabilizing at 1–3%

**Decision gate (end of Q2):**

**Is revenue growing month-over-month AND retention holding?**

- **Both yes** → you have a business; Q3 scales it.  
- **Retention yes, revenue flat** → monetization problem, not product problem. Fix paywall timing/pricing, push B2B harder (faster rupees than consumer).  
- **Revenue came only from 1 school deal, no consumer traction** → pivot toward B2B-first (this is a Plan C direction — see separate doc). Valid and often *more* profitable.

## Q3 — Widen Reach (≈ Jan–Mar 2027\)

**Build**

- **Audio \+ Hindi** — TTS instructions (en-IN → Hindi). Unlocks Grade 1 fully \+ tier 2/3 parents.  
- **iOS launch** (Capacitor makes it cheap) — captures the affluent, high-paying metro minority  
- Supporting character cast (v2 of Tinku universe)  
- A/B testing infrastructure (Remote Config) — test paywall, limits, notifications

**Distribution**

- Lean into Hindi-medium / tier 2/3 channels now that audio supports them  
- iOS opens App Store ASO as a second discovery surface  
- Referral mechanic ("invite a parent, both get a free month")  
- Scale whatever acquisition channel proved cheapest in Q2

**Target metrics**

- 10,000–30,000 cumulative installs  
- iOS contributing disproportionate revenue per user  
- Revenue: ₹40,000–1,50,000/month  
- A repeatable, positive-ROI acquisition channel identified

**Decision gate (end of Q3 / end of Year 1):**

**Is monthly revenue ≥ ₹50K AND is there a channel that brings users profitably?**

- **Yes** → Year 2 is a scale decision (see fork below). You've built a real thing.  
- **No, but retention strong & growing slowly** → lifestyle-business fork; stay lean, keep compounding, lower stress.  
- **No, and growth stalled** → serious strategic review; Plan B/C options become primary.

**Build**

**Distribution**

**Target metrics (Year-1 exit)**

## Year 2 — Expand & Deepen (FY 2027–28)

**Strategic theme: leverage the engine.** The skill-map architecture means new markets are cheap.

- **Second board** (big state board — Maharashtra SSC or UP Board, vernacular-led) — *higher ROI than a second subject* because the engine already does math; you just author a new skill map. This is the single biggest growth lever and the payoff of the architecture.  
- **Grades 6–8 "Challenger" band** — completes K-8, maximizes child lifetime  
- Possibly a **second subject** (EVS/Science) if math is saturated  
- **B2B becomes a revenue pillar** — likely the moment to register an **Indian entity** (contracts, UPI at scale, hiring)  
- Mature family plans, referral loops, retention automation

**Realistic range:** ₹2L–10L/month *if* Year-1 retention was strong. This is the year it becomes a real business or settles as a strong side project.

**Decision gate (end of Year 2):**

Scale fork or lifestyle fork — choose deliberately based on revenue trajectory, your appetite for team-building, and whether you want outside capital.

---

## Year 3 — Scale or Specialize (FY 2028–29)

**Fork A — Scale**

- Multiple boards \+ subjects, first hire (content/curriculum lead, NOT engineering — AI still covers code), real marketing budget, possibly fundraising  
- Target: a fundable or self-sustaining growth business

**Fork B — Lifestyle / niche dominance**

- Stay solo or tiny; own one niche (*the* CBSE primary-math app)  
- High margin, modest but reliable income, low stress  
- Completely valid — many of the best solo products live here

## The metrics dashboard (track these monthly, in one sheet)

| Metric | Why it matters | Healthy by Month 6 |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **D7 retention** | Predicts everything | ≥ 20% |
| **D30 retention** | Long-term stickiness | ≥ 8–10% |
| Installs (cumulative) | Reach | 5,000–10,000 |
| Free→paid conversion | Monetization health | 1–3% |
| Reviews / rating | ASO fuel | 50+ / ≥4.4 |
| Monthly revenue | Lagging indicator | ₹15K–60K |
| School accounts | B2B pillar | 1–5 |
| CAC (if paying for ads) | Channel viability | \< ₹40/install |

**If you track only one number: D7 retention.** It decides whether everything downstream is worth doing.

## The honest meta-point

This plan works only if you can sustain effort through the flat months 3–9, when work vastly exceeds reward. Your biggest risk is **stamina, not strategy.** Keep your Dubai runway separate, treat this as an 18-month compounding bet, and let the decision gates — not your mood — tell you when to push, fix, or pivot.

Plan B (retention/product failure) and Plan C (monetization/channel failure, incl. B2B-first pivot) are in the companion document.
